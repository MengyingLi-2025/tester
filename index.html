Grade 7 Chinese Character Dictation Competition - Term 3

Competition Overview

This competition aimed to reinforce students' memory of Chinese characters, encourage accurate handwriting and character recognition, and promote a fun and competitive learning environment through scoring, prizes, and public recognition.

Competition Objectives

Reinforce memory, encourage accuracy, foster competition

Duration

30-minute timed dictation challenge

Participants

All Grade 7 Chinese language learners

Competition Objectives

  • Reinforce students' memory of Chinese characters through a time-limited dictation challenge

  • Encourage accurate handwriting and character recognition abilities

  • Promote a fun and competitive learning environment through scoring, prizes, and public recognition

Competition Format

Task

Write as many correct Chinese characters from memory as possible within 30 minutes

Scoring

1 point per correct character. Bonus points for students scoring over 20 points

Recognition

  • Top 10 students across the grade received certificates and prizes
  • Top 3 classes (based on average score) received group certificates

Feedback & Suggestions

Strengths & Highlights

  • High Motivation

    High level of motivation and participation from all classes

  • Excellent Memorization

    Top performers demonstrated excellent character memorization abilities

  • Resource Utilization

    Active use of review materials and Pleco app for learning

Challenges & Issues

  • Stroke Order Errors

    Common mistakes included incorrect stroke order

  • Character Structure Issues

    Character structure errors, such as misplacement of radicals or components

  • Rote Memorization

    Some students relied too much on rote memorization without contextual understanding

  • Confusion with Similar Characters

    Some students confused similar-looking characters or forgot basic strokes under time pressure

Suggestions & Improvements

Classroom Improvements

  • Increase focus on character writing in class
  • Weekly practice dictation drills
  • Handwriting warm-up activities
  • Character decomposition and structure explanation

Learning Method Improvements

  • Teach radicals and how characters are formed
  • Highlight common confusion patterns
  • Gamification of writing practice
  • Team-based writing games

Incentive Measures

  • Stroke order competitions
  • Small incentives to reward progress

Digital Tool Integration

  • Interactive stroke order apps
  • Online character practice platforms
  • Multimedia learning resources

Conclusion & Future Plans

Competition Summary

The Chinese Character Writing Competition held on April 30, 2025, was a great success, with all 107 Grade 7 students actively participating. The competition effectively reinforced students' memory of Chinese characters and promoted a competitive learning environment.

Notable achievements include the high scores achieved by top students, with two students scoring a perfect 64 points. Class 2 performed exceptionally well, securing the first place with an average score of 21.80.

However, common errors such as stroke order mistakes and character structure issues were identified, highlighting areas for improvement in future teaching and learning strategies.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective Learning Tools

    Students who used review materials and digital tools like Pleco performed better

  • Importance of Structure

    Students need more practice and guidance on character structure and stroke order

  • Gamification Potential

    Incorporating games and competitions can significantly enhance motivation and engagement

Future Plans

  • Regular Practice Sessions

    Implement weekly dictation drills and handwriting warm-up activities

  • Radical-Based Learning

    Focus on teaching radicals and character composition to improve understanding

  • Digital Tool Integration

    Utilize interactive apps and online resources to supplement classroom learning

  • Future Competitions

    Organize more specialized competitions, such as stroke order competitions
